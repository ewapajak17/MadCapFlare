Exam for a sworn translator

The exam for a sworn translator consists of written and oral exam.

Written exam

In the written exam, candidates translate four texts (two texts from Polish into a foreign language and two texts from a foreign language into Polish). At least one of the texts is a court document, official letter or legal text. The written exam lasts four hours. In order to successfully pass the written part, a candidate needs to obtain 150 points (out of 200 points).

Note: Only candidates who pass the written exam are admitted to the oral exam. The oral exam takes place not earlier than 7 days after the written exam.

Oral exam

In the oral exam, candidates interpret two texts read by the speaker in Polish (consecutive interpreting) into a foreign language. Next, they receive two written texts in English, have 2 minutes to read each text and then they translate them orally into Polish (sight interpreting). Again, a candidate needs to obtain 150 points (out of 200 points) to pass the exam.
